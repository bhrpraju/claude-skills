⚡ Vague Prompt → Engineered Prompt

BEFORE — Vague
write me a linkedin post about AI
↓ Master Prompter reconstructs →
AFTER — Engineered
You are writing for a senior IT leader with 30+ years of experience, building their personal brand on LinkedIn. Audience: IT professionals, CXOs, program managers Tone: Direct, no hype, experience-backed Format: Hook (1 line) → 3-4 short paragraphs → CTA (1 line) Length: Max 180 words Topic: How AI automation is changing program management workflows — with a specific real-world example, not generic observations. Do NOT use: "In today's fast-paced world", "leverage", "game-changer", hashtag spam, or generic motivational conclusions.

📐 The REACT Framework

Every high-quality prompt needs all 5 components. Missing 2+ triggers auto-reconstruction.

R
Role
Who the AI is — persona, expertise, domain
E
Expected Output
Format, length, structure of the response
A
Audience
Who will read or use this output
C
Context
Background, constraints, data to use
T
Task
The actual ask — unambiguous, single goal

🚫 Anti-Patterns to Avoid

✗Vague quality words — "make it professional / good / better" → specify what "good" means
✗Instruction at the end — role + constraints BEFORE data, question AFTER
✗Over-asking — one prompt, one primary goal; split complex tasks
✗No format spec — always state expected output structure
✗Assuming context — state your role and situation explicitly
✗"Be creative" without guardrails — creativity without direction = inconsistent output
✗Conversational drift — re-anchor role and goal every 5–6 turns in long chats
